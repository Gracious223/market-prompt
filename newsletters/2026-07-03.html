Wall Street’s AI Race Is Creating a Crowding Crisis — Alpha Edges Collapse from Seven Years to 18 Months

Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today’s crowding risk story turns on concepts your analysts use daily to manage position sizing and risk. As AI accelerates alpha decay and amplifies herd behaviour in markets, these six terms are essential vocabulary for building products they’ll trust.

Crowded Trade
When many funds hold nearly identical positions. If they exit simultaneously, the selloff is amplified. AI adoption is accelerating crowding — today’s lead story is the clearest example in years.
Short Selling
Borrowing a security to sell it today, aiming to buy it back cheaper later and pocket the difference. Short sellers are often the first to identify overvaluation — including in crowded AI trades.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough percentage decline in a portfolio or asset value. Maximum drawdown is one of the core risk metrics portfolio managers use to evaluate a strategy — and what a crowded unwind can cause.
Systematic vs. Discretionary
Systematic funds trade by algorithm and rules; discretionary funds rely on human judgment. AI is blurring the line — and the crowding risk today is most acute in systematic funds running similar models.
Basis Points (bps)
One basis point = 1/100th of 1% (0.01%). The standard unit for expressing interest rate moves, credit spreads, and return differences. Analysts talk in bps — learn to think in them.
Market Capitalisation
Share price multiplied by shares outstanding. Determines index inclusion and weighting. Large-cap AI stocks dominate indices — which is why AI crowding now affects passive investors, not just hedge funds.
