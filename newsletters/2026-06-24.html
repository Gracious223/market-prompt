Public Markets

Markets Selloff

AI Stocks Sink for a Second Day as Wall Street Doubts the Payoff on AI Spending

The Nasdaq fell 2.21% and the S&P 500 dropped 1.44% on Tuesday, as investors sold off chipmakers and AI-infrastructure stocks. Nvidia fell about 4% and Alphabet (Google's parent company) fell about 5%. Memory-chip maker Micron dropped 13% ahead of its earnings report, as a price collapse in memory chips (the chips that store data for AI systems) spread from South Korea's stock market into U.S. tech stocks.

When chip stocks and AI-adjacent names fall together like this, it's a signal that big investors are starting to ask whether the hundreds of billions companies are spending on AI infrastructure will ever turn into profit — the same question your own AI-feature business cases need to be able to answer.

AI Valuations

Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI as the World's Most Valuable Private AI Company

Anthropic (the maker of the Claude AI models) raised $65 billion at a $965 billion valuation (what investors think the whole company is worth) — pushing it past OpenAI's $852 billion valuation from earlier this year. Anthropic says its run-rate revenue (a year's revenue estimated from recent months) has reached $47 billion, up from $30 billion at the start of 2026. Both companies have now confidentially filed paperwork to prepare for an eventual IPO (selling shares to the public for the first time).

If Anthropic goes public, it will have to publish real, audited financial statements — the first hard numbers anyone outside the company will have on what it actually costs to run a frontier AI lab, and a useful benchmark for testing your own AI cost and margin assumptions against.

Tools for Analysts & PMs

Research Tools

Bloomberg Rolls Out Its First AI Chatbot, ASKB, to Every Terminal User

Bloomberg began rolling out ASKB, a conversational AI tool built into the Bloomberg Terminal (the data and trading platform used by most professional investors), to all of its users this month. Analysts can now ask plain-English questions about a stock or company and get an answer pulled directly from Bloomberg's own news, data, and research, instead of searching across separate screens.

This is a production version of the workflow most AI PMs are trying to design from scratch: synthesis built directly into the tool analysts already trust and already have open, not a separate chatbot they have to remember to launch.

Agentic Finance

An $18 Billion Hedge Fund Is Building a Fund Where AI Agents — Not Analysts — Do the Research

Magnetar Capital, an $18 billion hedge fund, is building a fund where hundreds of AI agents (software that can research and take multi-step actions on its own) source ideas, analyze companies, and forecast trends — with humans only approving the final trade. Separately, Morgan Stanley is opening its $1.2 trillion workplace wealth platform so corporate clients' own AI agents can pull account data directly, instead of a human logging in.

Notice where the human stays in both cases: approving the trade, not running the analysis. That human-approval checkpoint is the design pattern regulators are already starting to expect by default — see Watch Closely, below.

From the Labs

Talent War

Google DeepMind Loses Two Star Researchers to Rivals Within Days of Each Other

Noam Shazeer — a co-author of the "Attention Is All You Need" paper that introduced the transformer architecture underlying nearly all of today's AI models — left Google DeepMind for OpenAI on June 18. Days later, John Jumper, who won the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for AlphaFold (an AI system that predicts the 3D shape of proteins), announced he's leaving DeepMind for Anthropic after nearly nine years.

Where the most credentialed researchers choose to work is a leading indicator of where a lab's next breakthrough — and next product advantage — is likely to come from. It's worth tracking the same way you'd track executive departures at a public company.

Frontier Models

OpenAI Launches an AI Model Built to Find and Patch Security Flaws

OpenAI released GPT-5.5-Cyber, a version of its model tuned for cybersecurity work and restricted to vetted security teams. Alongside it, OpenAI launched "Patch the Planet," a program with security firms Trail of Bits and HackerOne that uses AI to fix vulnerabilities in widely-used open-source software such as cURL, Go, and Python.

This is OpenAI building a concrete, sellable enterprise product around a specific capability rather than a general chat feature — the kind of vertical, workflow-specific bet your own roadmap decisions should be benchmarked against.

AI Product Management

Role & Career

New Industry Report: the Classic "Product Manager" Job Won't Exist by 2030

A survey of more than 1,500 product leaders, published by Products That Count and venture firm Mighty Capital, found that traditional Product Manager job postings have already fallen 30% this year — and up to 70% at software companies — while a new hybrid role, the "Product Builder" (part PM, part engineer), grew 10x over the same period.

If you aren't already building enough technical fluency to prototype or evaluate AI features yourself, this report is a concrete data point for why that gap will matter to your own career, not just an abstract industry trend.

Watch Closely

Regulation

Global Regulators Start Writing the Rulebook for AI Agents in Finance

The Financial Stability Board (which coordinates financial regulation across G20 countries) published draft "sound practices" for AI in finance on June 10, specifically flagging the risk of autonomous AI agents acting without enough human oversight, and recommending firms use AI to monitor their own AI. Separately, the Bank of England, the FCA, and HM Treasury jointly warned in May that today's frontier AI models can already find and exploit cyberattack vulnerabilities faster and more cheaply than skilled human hackers. (No new posts on Anthropic's own Alignment Science blog since May 8 — we checked.)

Expect regulators to require a documented human-checkpoint and monitoring layer around any AI agent your firm deploys, not just a good accuracy score. Tuesday's multi-hour Claude outage, which hit all Anthropic models worldwide at once, is a timely reminder that the same monitoring discipline applies to vendor reliability, not just model behavior.
