Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

As the AI equity trade rotates and your portfolio managers re-evaluate exposure, fluency with how markets are structured and measured lets you participate credibly in those conversations — and build products that reflect how analysts actually think about risk and valuation. Six core building blocks for today.

Market Capitalisation
Share price multiplied by shares outstanding. Defines whether a company is large-cap (>$10B), mid-cap ($2–10B), or small-cap (<$2B). The AI trade rotation from Mag 7 to semiconductors is a large-to-large-cap rotation within AI, not a cap-size shift.
P/E Ratio
Price divided by earnings per share — how much investors pay for each £1 of profit. High P/E signals growth expectations. Many AI infrastructure names trade at elevated P/Es because the market is pricing in future earnings, not current ones.
Liquidity
How easily an asset can be bought or sold without moving the price. High liquidity = tight bid-ask spreads and fast execution. AI tools that surface illiquid positions or liquidity mismatches in a portfolio are among the most valued risk applications on the buy side.
Beta
A stock's sensitivity to market movements. Beta > 1 moves more than the index; < 1 moves less. Many AI and semiconductor names carry high beta — they amplify both gains and selloffs. Analysts use beta to model how an AI-heavy portfolio behaves in risk-off markets.
ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)
A basket of securities traded like a single stock. AI-themed ETFs (e.g. tracking semiconductor or robotics indices) now represent significant passive exposure to the AI infrastructure trade — and create correlation risks when rebalancing flows move in the same direction at the same time.
Bid-Ask Spread
The difference between the highest price a buyer will pay (bid) and the lowest a seller will accept (ask). A measure of market liquidity and trading cost. AI market-making models are increasingly used by electronic trading desks to optimise spread capture across thousands of names simultaneously.

The AI-Agent Era in Financial Services Has Arrived — 44% of Finance Teams Deploying Agents by Year-End

The ABA Banking Journal reports that agentic AI — systems that plan, reason, and execute multi-step tasks autonomously — is entering production in financial services for portfolio monitoring, fraud detection, and compliance automation. Industry data shows 44% of finance teams will deploy agentic AI by end of 2026: a greater than 600% year-over-year rise from a very low 2025 base.

Why It Matters

Most firms have the intent but not the internal capability — that gap is your product opportunity, and it is closing faster than most internal roadmaps have anticipated. The question is no longer whether to build agentic products, but which decisions your agents are permitted to own.

Research & Think About

  • Research the concept of "human-in-the-loop" versus "human-on-the-loop" — the distinction defines how much an agent can act before requiring human approval, and it sits at the centre of every regulated agentic deployment
  • Draft: for one workflow your analysts currently own manually, map where an agent could take over each step — and where a human approval gate must remain

FCA · Model Providers

FCA Seeks Powers to Regulate OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Directly as Critical Financial Infrastructure

The FCA is seeking expanded authority to supervise large language model providers — including OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Microsoft — as they provide financial guidance directly to consumers without regulatory protection. The proposal would bring model providers under the Critical Third Parties regime, imposing direct supervisory obligations on the underlying models, not just the firms deploying them.

Why It Matters

If Anthropic and OpenAI become regulated critical third parties in the UK, your vendor selection and contract terms will carry regulatory weight your procurement team is not yet equipped to assess. This is a governance and due-diligence question for your next PRD, not just a news item.

Research & Think About
  • Read BanklessTimes's July 6 piece and the ITPro coverage — both clearly explain what the Critical Third Parties regime actually requires of designated firms, which is the detail that matters for your product
  • Research what the FCA's Critical Third Parties regime currently requires of cloud providers like AWS and Azure — your AI vendor's obligations under an expanded regime would mirror this
  • Ask your legal team: if Anthropic were designated a critical third party, what would change in your data processing agreement, SLA requirements, and incident reporting obligations?

Adoption · Measurement

Cambridge CCAF: 81% of Financial Firms Adopt AI — But 55% Cannot Measure the Value They're Capturing

The Cambridge Centre for Alternative Finance's 2026 Global AI in Financial Services report finds 81% of firms adopting AI, only 40% at advanced stage, and 55% unable to measure business value captured. Data privacy (flagged by 73% of respondents) and model hallucinations are top risks; legacy architecture and data quality are the leading barriers to scale.

Why It Matters

The measurement gap is your product's opportunity — and its biggest threat. If your internal stakeholders cannot measure what your tool delivers, they will cut it before they fix it. Embedding measurable outcomes into your product design is now a survival question, not a nice-to-have.

Research & Think About
  • Download the full CCAF 2026 report PDF at jbs.cam.ac.uk — it is free and the most rigorous primary source on where financial services AI actually is versus where marketing says it is
  • For your current product: write down three specific, measurable outcomes that would demonstrate value to a sceptical CFO — time saved, decisions improved, errors reduced. If you cannot write them clearly, your users probably cannot measure them either
  • Research "AI ROI measurement frameworks" — the CCAF report identifies this as the industry's central unsolved problem, and the PM who can help their firm solve it has a significant internal influence advantage
