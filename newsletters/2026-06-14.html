Public Markets

Lead Story

Goldman and Morgan Stanley Wage a $7B Battle Over Who Banks the AI IPOs

Anthropic tapped Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan as lead underwriters on 3 June; OpenAI is expected to follow soon. Both IPOs could raise $60B+ each — over 40% more than SpaceX's raise. The prize is "lead left": the bank that controls share allocation and pockets the largest fee share.

The banks advising Anthropic on its own IPO will also be the desks producing research coverage on it afterward. If your firm's analysts read that research, the same conflict-of-interest questions you'd apply to any sell-side note now apply to coverage of your model vendor.

Market Volatility

AI Chip Stocks Take Another Beating — AMD and Intel Both Drop Over 10% in a Day

On 4 June, AMD fell 10.86% and Intel 11.28% in a single session as chipmakers led a fresh leg down in the AI-fuelled selloff. Since record highs on 2 June, the Nasdaq is down roughly 5% and the S&P 500 about 3%, with hyperscalers also sliding.

Two double-digit single-day moves in core AI infrastructure names within a week is a volatility regime, not noise. If your product surfaces market-data signals to PMs, this is exactly what it needs to contextualize, not just report.

Industry Research

New Cambridge Report: Finance Leads on AI Adoption, Lags on Managing Its Risks

The 2026 Global AI in Financial Services Report finds 81% of firms have adopted AI, with fintechs (47%) far ahead of incumbents (30%) on advanced use cases. Data privacy tops concerns for 73% of respondents — and the industry is now ahead of its own regulators on adoption.

Your firm is plausibly in the "incumbent" 30%, not the fintech 47%. The report's framing — that industry has outpaced regulators — is exactly the gap your product's governance design needs to close before a regulator asks.

Agentic AI

Wealth Management

Morgan Stanley Will Let Outside AI Agents Log Into Its Wealth Platforms

Morgan Stanley plans to give corporate clients' AI agents direct access to its ShareWorks and Equity Edge stock-plan platforms, bypassing human-facing interfaces entirely. A handful of clients have early access now; its 3,400 administration clients follow next year.

This is the first major bank admitting its UI is becoming a secondary interface — agents are the primary user. If your internal tools are still designed only for humans clicking through screens, you're behind where Morgan Stanley's own clients will be within a year.

Infrastructure

Experian Launches an "Agent Operating System" for Regulated Finance

Unveiled at Money20/20 Europe on 2 June, Experian's Agent Operating System lets a firm's own agents, Experian's agents, and partner agents work together through a shared trust and orchestration layer — with built-in auditability and human-oversight controls, covering lending from acquisition to credit decisioning.

Experian is productizing exactly the governance layer regulators keep asking for. "Auditability and human oversight built into the orchestration layer" could appear in your own PRD — worth seeing how a vendor actually implements it.

Regulation

SEC · US

SEC's 2026 Exam Priorities Put AI Disclosure Accuracy Under the Microscope

The SEC's 2026 exam priorities flag investment advisers' AI use as a focus area — examiners will check whether firms' AI disclosures match what their tools actually do, and whether adequate policies exist to supervise AI use, even as Biden-era AI-specific rule proposals stay withdrawn.

"Does your disclosure match what the tool actually does" is a question your product documentation should already answer. Examiners checking this externally means compliance will be asking you this internally, soon.

Data Protection

Reg S-P's AI-Vendor Data Rules Just Hit Smaller Firms

3 June was the compliance deadline for smaller firms under the SEC's amended Regulation S-P, which tightens safeguards for customer data — including data shared with third-party vendors. Any AI tool touching client or analyst data now falls inside this perimeter.

If your product sends analyst queries or portfolio data to an external model provider, you are the "third-party vendor" Reg S-P now regulates your firm's relationship with — even if you built the product internally.

From the Labs

Microsoft

Microsoft Ships Its First In-House Coding Model — Built on Copilot's Own Usage Data

MAI-Code-1-Flash, a 5B-parameter model trained on GitHub Copilot's real editing, terminal, and multi-step task data, began rolling out to all Copilot tiers on 2 June. It scores 51% on SWE-Bench Pro using up to 60% fewer tokens than comparable models.

A small model competitive with much larger ones, trained on real usage logs rather than generic data, is the template for "firm-aware" AI (see Two Sigma, last issue). Your product's usage logs are a training asset — are they being captured anywhere?

Apple / Google

Apple Hands Siri to Google — and Quietly Makes Claude an Option Too

At WWDC on 8 June, Apple unveiled a rebuilt Siri running on a custom Gemini model under a roughly $1B/year Google deal, plus visual intelligence and persistent memory. iOS 27 also lets users set Claude or ChatGPT as their default assistant via Extensions.

Apple just made "pick your model provider" a consumer setting on a billion devices. If switching AI providers is becoming this normalized for consumers, your analysts will expect the same flexibility from internal tools.
