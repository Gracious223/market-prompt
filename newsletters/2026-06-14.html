Lead Story

Goldman and Morgan Stanley Wage a $7B Battle Over Who Banks the AI IPOs

Anthropic tapped Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan as lead underwriters on 3 June; OpenAI is expected to follow soon. Both IPOs could raise $60B+ each — over 40% more than SpaceX's raise. The prize is "lead left": the bank that controls share allocation and pockets the largest fee share.

Why It Matters

The banks advising Anthropic on its own IPO will also be the desks producing research coverage on it afterward. If your firm's analysts read that research, the same conflict-of-interest questions you'd apply to any sell-side note now apply to coverage of your model vendor.

Research & Think About
  • Ask compliance: does your firm have a policy for covering or holding stock in AI vendors it also relies on operationally?
  • Read up on "Chinese walls" between underwriting and research divisions — how robust are they in practice?
  • Compare Anthropic's reported $965B target valuation (see last issue) to its revenue — what does that imply for post-IPO analyst price targets?

Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

This week's Goldman-vs-Morgan-Stanley story is really an IPO mechanics story. Here are six terms that explain what the banks are actually fighting over — and what happens once Anthropic and OpenAI shares start trading.

Book Runner
A bank managing a share offering — building the order book, marketing to investors, and pricing the deal. Multiple banks can be book runners; only one is "lead left."
Lead Left
The underwriter listed first on the prospectus cover — controls allocation of shares to investors and takes the largest fee share. The role Goldman and Morgan Stanley are fighting over.
Underwriting Syndicate
The group of banks collectively distributing an IPO's shares. Junior members get smaller allocations and fees but still provide analyst coverage afterward.
Roadshow / Bookbuilding
The pre-IPO process where bankers and management pitch institutional investors, gauge demand, and set the price range before shares are allocated.
Lock-Up Period
A contractual window (often 90-180 days) after an IPO during which insiders and early investors can't sell shares — relevant once Anthropic and OpenAI employees can finally cash out.
Greenshoe Option
An over-allotment option letting underwriters sell up to 15% more shares than planned if demand is high, helping stabilize the price after listing.

Morgan Stanley Will Let Outside AI Agents Log Into Its Wealth Platforms

Morgan Stanley plans to give corporate clients' AI agents direct access to its ShareWorks and Equity Edge stock-plan platforms, bypassing human-facing interfaces entirely. A handful of clients have early access now; its 3,400 administration clients follow next year.

Why It Matters

This is the first major bank admitting its UI is becoming a secondary interface — agents are the primary user. If your internal tools are still designed only for humans clicking through screens, you're behind where Morgan Stanley's own clients will be within a year.

Research & Think About
  • Sketch what an "agent-first" version of your product's interface would need: API access, structured outputs, auth for non-human users
  • Ask: who is accountable if a client's external agent misreads data pulled from your firm's platform?
  • Read Mark Mitchell's framing in full — compare it to how your firm talks about "agentic" internally

Data Protection

Reg S-P's AI-Vendor Data Rules Just Hit Smaller Firms

3 June was the compliance deadline for smaller firms under the SEC's amended Regulation S-P, which tightens safeguards for customer data — including data shared with third-party vendors. Any AI tool touching client or analyst data now falls inside this perimeter.

Why It Matters

If your product sends analyst queries or portfolio data to an external model provider, you are the "third-party vendor" Reg S-P now regulates your firm's relationship with — even if you built the product internally.

Research & Think About
  • Map every place your product sends data to an external AI provider — does each have a data processing agreement?
  • Read the Reg S-P amendment text on third-party vendor oversight — what documentation does it require?
  • Ask: would your product's data flows survive an incident-notification requirement under Reg S-P?

MAI-Code-1-Flash, a 5B-parameter model trained on GitHub Copilot's real editing, terminal, and multi-step task data, began rolling out to all Copilot tiers on 2 June. It scores 51% on SWE-Bench Pro using up to 60% fewer tokens than comparable models.
