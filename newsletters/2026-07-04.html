Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Your analysts and portfolio managers speak in terms of yield, liquidity, hedges, and valuations — daily, automatically. Building vocabulary in these areas is how you earn credibility and ask the right questions in the room. Six more terms to add to your working knowledge.

Yield Curve
A chart showing bond yields across different maturities. An inverted yield curve — where short-term rates exceed long-term — has historically signalled recession. Macro analysts watch it as a leading credit indicator.
Liquidity
How easily an asset can be bought or sold without significantly moving its price. Illiquid assets are harder to exit under stress. AI tools that surface real-time liquidity risk are high-value for portfolio managers.
Hedging
Using derivatives (options, futures, swaps) to reduce exposure to a specific risk — e.g. FX risk or interest rate moves — while retaining upside in other positions. Hedging decisions are core to how PMs manage portfolios.
Price-to-Earnings (P/E)
A stock's price divided by its annual earnings per share. A high P/E implies investors expect future growth. AI companies currently trade at P/E levels with no historical precedent — a key challenge for analysts.
Passive vs. Active
Passive funds track an index (e.g. S&P 500) at low cost; active managers attempt to beat the market through stock selection. AI is increasingly blurring this distinction via factor-based and systematic strategies.
Net Asset Value (NAV)
The per-unit value of a fund — total assets minus liabilities — calculated daily. NAV is how mutual funds and ETFs price their units. A foundational metric your analysts reference every trading day.
