Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Six terms your analysts use daily — drawn from today's stories. Each one connects to a real decision your product must support.

Benchmark Index
A standard reference — like the S&P 500 or Nasdaq — that measures overall market performance. Portfolio managers' results are compared against it. "Beating the benchmark" is the core objective.
ADR (American Depositary Receipt)
A legal structure that lets foreign companies (like SK Hynix) sell shares directly to U.S. investors on American exchanges, without the complexity of a full U.S. incorporation.
Market Capitalisation
Share price × total shares outstanding. SK Hynix crossed $1 trillion — putting it in the club of the world's most valuable public companies. It signals scale and investor conviction.
Earnings Season
The four-week period after each quarter when public companies report financial results. Analysts use this as high-signal time — guidance updates and surprises move markets significantly.
Sector Rotation
When investors move capital from one sector (e.g. consumer goods) to another (e.g. technology). Q2 saw a rotation into AI infrastructure as growth expectations shifted upward.
Short Interest
The share of a company's stock that has been borrowed and sold short — a bet it will fall. High short interest in AI names (like SMCI) signals some investors doubt the hype can sustain.

IOSCO published its Supervisory Toolkit for AI Use in Capital Markets on May 25; the comment period closed June 26.
