Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today's terms cover the analytical vocabulary your users reach for every day — from how funds are valued to how risk-adjusted performance is measured. These concepts come up constantly when analysts assess AI tool outputs and when PMs scope what their products need to do.

NAV (Net Asset Value)
The per-share value of a fund: total assets minus liabilities, divided by shares outstanding. Fund managers live by NAV — it's how performance is measured and how investors price in or out.
P/E Ratio
Price-to-Earnings. Tells you how much investors pay for each £1 of profit. A high P/E signals high growth expectations; a low P/E can mean value or distress. Equity analysts use this constantly in stock screening.
Sharpe Ratio
Risk-adjusted return: how much excess return a portfolio earns per unit of risk taken. Higher is better. It's how PMs justify their strategy — AI tools that improve Sharpe ratios are immediately compelling.
Derivatives
Financial contracts whose value derives from an underlying asset (e.g. options on equities, interest rate swaps). Used for hedging and speculation. AI is increasingly used to price and risk-manage complex derivatives books.
ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)
A fund that trades on an exchange like a stock, tracking an index or basket of assets. ETFs have grown to represent a huge share of daily equity volume — AI tools are used to analyse flows and detect sentiment shifts.
Yield Curve
A chart of interest rates across different maturities (e.g. 2-year vs. 10-year government bonds). An inverted yield curve (short rates above long rates) has historically signalled recession — macro analysts watch this daily.
