Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Six more terms your analysts use daily — each one changes how you frame a product requirement or a data problem. Build this vocabulary and you stop needing a translator in the room.

Short Selling
Borrowing a security and selling it, expecting to buy it back cheaper later. It lets markets express negative conviction. AI tools that surface short interest data show where professionals are betting against a position.
Basis Points (bps)
One basis point = 0.01%. Used everywhere: interest rates, credit spreads, management fees. When the Fed cuts 25bps, that is a 0.25% move. You will hear this daily — fluency matters.
Yield Curve
A graph of bond yields plotted against time to maturity. Normally upward sloping. An inverted curve — where short rates exceed long rates — has historically preceded recessions. Macro-sensitive portfolios watch this closely.
P/E Ratio
Price-to-Earnings: how much investors pay per dollar of annual profit. A P/E of 40 means you pay £40 for £1 of earnings. AI stocks often trade at 60–100x — implying significant future growth expectations.
Liquidity
How easily an asset can be bought or sold without moving its price. Large-cap equities are highly liquid. Private credit is not. Your product's data feeds are only as useful as the markets they can actually cover.
AUM (Assets Under Management)
The total market value of assets a fund controls. A £10B AUM fund manages £10 billion of client capital. AUM drives fee income — and determines how much a PM cares about execution costs and slippage.

Ant Murphy: Product Is Changing in 2026 — Judgment Is the Last Skill AI Can't Replace

Murphy's widely-shared analysis argues the PMs pulling ahead in 2026 are not the most tool-fluent. The common failure mode: using AI to move faster at work you would have done anyway, instead of directing it toward work AI genuinely cannot do. Strategy, context-setting, and judgment under uncertainty remain purely human. The PM who outsources those loses the room.

Why It Matters

This is the direct challenge to "I'll use AI to write my PRDs faster." The harder question is whether AI changes what should be in the PRD at all — and your analysts are the only people who can tell you the answer.

Research & Think About
