Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Your analysts and portfolio managers use these terms without explanation — often in the same sentence. Fluency here is what lets you ask better questions in rooms where the stakes are high. Six more terms to add to your working vocabulary.

Basis Points (bps)
One hundredth of a percentage point (0.01%). A rate moving from 4.50% to 4.75% is a 25 bps rise. PMs and analysts speak in bps daily — it removes ambiguity about whether “half a percent” is 0.5% or 50%.
Duration
A bond’s sensitivity to interest rate changes. A bond with duration of 7 loses roughly 7% in value for every 1% rise in rates. AI tools that model duration risk across large portfolios in real time are among the most sought-after in fixed income.
Earnings Per Share (EPS)
Net profit divided by shares outstanding. EPS is how analysts track whether a company is growing profitability on a per-share basis. Beating or missing consensus EPS estimates is often the biggest single driver of a stock’s post-earnings move.
Market Capitalisation
Share price multiplied by total shares outstanding — the market’s valuation of a company. Large-cap (>$10B), mid-cap ($2–10B), small-cap (<$2B) describe scale. Analysts often specialise by cap size; your product may need to serve each differently.
Short Selling
Borrowing and selling a security you don’t own, expecting the price to fall so you can buy it back cheaper. Short sellers are often the first to surface fraud or overvaluation. AI tools that monitor short interest are used as contrarian signals by sophisticated PMs.
Credit Spread
The yield difference between a corporate bond and a comparable government bond. A widening spread signals rising perceived risk. Spread compression — tightening — signals confidence. AI systems that monitor real-time spread movements are core infrastructure in fixed income teams.

CFA Institute: 85% Want Agentic AI — Fewer Than 25% Have the Governance to Run It

CFA Institute research on agentic AI in finance finds 85% of businesses want to operate as agentic enterprises and nearly three-quarters plan to deploy autonomous agents. Why It Matters

The governance gap is your product opportunity — and your liability exposure. Building agent guardrails, audit trails, and human escalation checkpoints into your architecture from the start is a product decision, not a compliance afterthought.

FCA Chief: Agentic AI Is a “Profound Step Change” — Payments Rules May Be Rewritten

FCA Chief Executive Nikhil Rathi described agentic AI as a “profound step change” at a techUK event in June 2026. The regulator signalled it will consider whether payments regulation needs to be rewritten to accommodate agentic systems — the first time the FCA has indicated it may go beyond applying existing frameworks to new technology.

Why It Matters

The FCA’s consistent position has been “apply existing rules to AI.” A signal that new rules may be needed for agentic payments is a significant shift. It means your product’s regulatory surface may change materially — not just how current rules apply, but whether new ones are coming.

The PM Role Is Redefining Around Orchestration — Not Feature Management

2026 analysis from the Institute of AI Product Management finds the biggest structural change in the PM role: agentic AI is replacing simple chatbot features, MCP is standardising how AI connects to tools, and “vibe coding” lets PMs prototype without engineers. The role is shifting from feature manager to AI system orchestrator — faster than most PM career paths anticipated.

Why It Matters

Orchestration means understanding how agents interact with tools, data, and humans — not just specifying requirements. If your mental model of the PM role is still primarily about feature prioritisation, the gap between where you are and where the role is going is widening every quarter.

Research & Think About
