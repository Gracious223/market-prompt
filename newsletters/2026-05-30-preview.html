Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Public markets are where publicly listed securities — equities, bonds, derivatives — are bought and sold. Your internal users (portfolio managers and analysts) operate across these asset classes daily. Here is the vocabulary you need to work credibly alongside them.

Buy-Side vs. Sell-Side
Buy-side (your users) manage capital — funds, pension pots, insurance assets. Sell-side (banks, brokers) provide research and execution services to buy-side firms.
Alpha
Return above a benchmark index. A portfolio manager generating "alpha" is beating the market. AI tools are most valuable when they help find alpha faster or more consistently.
Investment Thesis
A structured argument for why an asset is mispriced. It includes a catalyst, valuation, risk factors, and a time horizon. Analysts spend significant time building and stress-testing these.
IG vs. HY Bonds
Investment-Grade bonds are rated BBB- or higher — lower risk, lower yield. High-Yield (junk) bonds are riskier, higher yield. AI now drives ~50% of IG issuance.
Factor Investing
Targeting returns based on specific characteristics (value, momentum, quality). AI is being used to identify and combine factors at speed humans cannot match.
Portfolio Construction
The process of selecting and weighting assets to achieve a risk/return target. AI tools that support construction decisions are among the highest-value applications in your space.
