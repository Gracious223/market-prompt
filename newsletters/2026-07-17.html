JPMorgan's AI Agents Beat the 60/40 — and the Strategist Says Don't Trust It

All eight of JPMorgan's AI agents outperformed a traditional 60/40 portfolio on a risk-adjusted basis in 20-year backtests. The agents classified markets into four regimes — Goldilocks, reflation, stagflation, risk-off — and dynamically reallocated across asset classes. The best-performing system beat the 60/40 by 0.7 percentage points annually with lower volatility. But strategist Thomas Salopek published the caveat alongside the result: "We strongly caution against uncritically accepting what amounts to in-sample, overly confident answers of AI."

Why It Matters

Your analysts will encounter this research. The key product question isn't "can AI beat markets?" — it's how do you build trust and auditability into AI outputs that inform high-stakes decisions? JPMorgan's own caveat is the brief for your next PRD.

Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today's top story turns on market regimes and risk-adjusted returns — the vocabulary JPMorgan's research uses throughout. Here are six terms that will help you read and discuss this research credibly with portfolio managers.

Market Regime
A recurring macroeconomic environment characterised by growth and inflation dynamics. JPMorgan's AI agents classified markets into four: Goldilocks (high growth, low inflation), Reflation, Stagflation, and Risk-Off. Regime detection is a core use case for AI in macro investing.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough decline in a portfolio's value over a given period. A strategy with "lower volatility and lower drawdown" is more consistent — it loses less in bad periods. Your PM users track maximum drawdown as a key risk metric for any strategy.
TRACE
Trade Reporting and Compliance Engine — FINRA's database for US corporate bond transactions. It makes fixed income pricing transparent and is a key data source for AI tools serving bond investors. Coalition Greenwich specifically cited it as a competitive differentiator.
Credit Spread
The yield difference between a corporate bond and a risk-free government bond of similar maturity. A widening spread signals higher perceived default risk. AI tools that surface real-time spread data help fixed income analysts spot stress signals faster than traditional screens.
Book Value
A company's net asset value as recorded on its balance sheet — total assets minus total liabilities. Analysts compare Book Value to Market Cap to assess whether a stock is over- or under-valued. AI tools are increasingly used to flag divergences at scale across large universes.
Machine-Readable Research
Broker research structured as data (JSON, XML, tagged PDFs) rather than plain documents, enabling AI to ingest and process it automatically. 77% of buy-side firms told Cambridge they want broker research delivered in this format — it's a product surface you should be thinking about.

FCA Publishes Landmark Mills Review — 7 Priority Recommendations for Agentic Finance

The FCA's Sheldon Mills published his landmark review on 6 July. Key finding: AI will structurally reshape retail financial services by 2030 across four dimensions — firm operations, consumer journeys, market competition, and fraud risk. Why It Matters
