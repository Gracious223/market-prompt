AI in Public Markets

Financial Services

AI Now Accounts for Nearly Half of All Investment-Grade Debt Issuance

Apollo's research arm reported this week that AI-related issuers now represent nearly 50% of all IG bond issuance and 87% of VC funding — a signal that the AI investment cycle has moved well beyond hype into structural capital allocation. For portfolio managers, this is no longer a thematic tilt; it's the base case.

Why it matters for you: Your internal users are making decisions in a market where AI is the dominant force. Understanding this shapes what data and signals your product should surface to them.

Wealth Management

Vanguard Ships "Expert Insights" — AI Portfolio Analysis for Advisors

Vanguard launched a production AI tool that transforms complex portfolio data into client-ready guidance, aligned with their investment methodology. It will be embedded into the free Portfolio Analytics Tool later in 2026. This is one of the clearest examples yet of an incumbent shipping an AI product that's genuinely workflow-integrated — not a chatbot bolted on the side.

Why it matters for you: Study this product closely — Vanguard's approach to making AI output "client-ready" and "aligned to methodology" is exactly the trust and explainability problem you'll face building for your internal analysts.

Tools for Analysts & PMs

Research Tools

AlphaSense Evolves into a Full Research Agent

AlphaSense's January 2026 Generative Search update transformed the platform from a search tool into a full research agent — capable of answering multi-step questions and producing structured deliverables by combining quantitative data with broker research, expert calls, and filings. Hedge funds and buy-side analysts are the primary users.

Why it matters for you: This is a direct competitor blueprint. AlphaSense shows what "good" looks like for an AI product serving analysts — the key is unifying qualitative and quantitative sources in one conversational interface, not just adding chat to existing tools.

From the Labs

Google I/O

Google Bets on Speed — Gemini 3.5 Flash Over a Frontier Behemoth

At Google I/O this week, Google debuted Gemini 3.5 Flash rather than a heavyweight frontier model, signalling a deliberate strategy: stay at the frontier while prioritising models cheap and fast enough to deploy across billions of users. Search is being overhauled for both short queries and chatbot-style conversations. YouTube is getting "Ask YouTube."

Why it matters for you: Speed and cost-efficiency are becoming the real competitive moat in AI — not raw capability. When you're scoping your product, latency and cost per query matter as much as model quality to your users.

AI Product Management

Career & Role

The PM Role Is Splitting — Generalists Are Being Squeezed Out

94% of product professionals now use AI frequently, with nearly half embedding it deeply into daily workflows. The result: generalist PM roles are under pressure as companies increasingly demand specialisation — AI-native PMs, growth PMs, platform PMs. The PMs gaining ground are those reallocating reclaimed hours toward product vision and customer empathy: the tasks AI cannot replicate.

Why it matters for you: You are making exactly the right move transitioning into AI PM specialisation now. The market is rewarding this shift. The risk to avoid: becoming a requirements-passer rather than a strategic leader with real AI product depth.

Watch Closely

Risk

IMF Flags AI-Fuelled Cyberattacks as a Financial Stability Risk

The IMF raised formal concerns this week about AI's role in destabilising financial systems — advanced models can now dramatically reduce the time and cost needed to identify and exploit vulnerabilities across widely used systems. Separately, a Cambridge Judge Business School report found that accuracy of AI-generated financial outputs (48%) and cyber threats (50%) are now the top two concerns among financial firms deploying AI.

Why it matters for you: When your internal analysts rely on AI outputs to make investment decisions, accuracy and auditability are non-negotiable product requirements — not nice-to-haves. Build these into your PRD from day one.
