Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today's lead story makes direct use of six concepts your portfolio managers live by. Understanding these is the vocabulary you need to build tools they will trust.

Market Regime
A classification of prevailing market conditions — Goldilocks, reflation, stagflation, risk-off — used to guide asset allocation. JPMorgan's AI agents shift between stocks and bonds based on regime signals.
Risk-Adjusted Return
Performance measured relative to the risk taken to achieve it. Beating the 60/40 "on a risk-adjusted basis" means more return for each unit of volatility, not just higher raw return.
Backtest
Testing an investment strategy against historical data to evaluate past performance. Critical caveat: backtests are in-sample and almost always overstate live performance — JPMorgan says this explicitly.
60/40 Portfolio
The classic balanced allocation: 60% equities, 40% bonds. Long considered the default for institutional portfolios, it is now the benchmark AI agents are measured against in performance tests.
Sharpe Ratio
A measure of excess return per unit of risk (standard deviation). A higher Sharpe means better risk-adjusted returns — the standard metric for evaluating whether an AI portfolio strategy is worth deploying.
Multistrategy Fund
A hedge fund running multiple uncorrelated strategies — equity, macro, quant, fixed income — simultaneously. Firms like Millennium and Schonfeld run these platforms; smaller concentrated funds outperformed them in H1 2026.
