No Financial Modeler Trusts AI for High-Stakes Decisions — Even as 86% Use It Daily

The Financial Modeling Institute surveyed 63 financial modeling professionals from 26 countries in its landmark "The Human Financial Modeller" report (7 July 2026). Despite 86% using AI tools in the past year, not a single expert would rely on an AI-generated financial model for high-stakes business decisions without independent human review. Seventy percent use AI for less than 25% of their workflow, and nearly half report little or no measurable time savings.

Why It Matters

This is the trust gap your product must close. Your analysts are in this 86% — using AI but not trusting it. Explainability, auditability, and human-in-the-loop design are not differentiators; they are the minimum viable product for buy-side adoption.

  • Ask: what would have to be true about your product's outputs for a PM to act on them without a second check?
  • Research "model cards" and "AI datasheets" — disclosure formats that build trust through transparency

Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

As your product surfaces signals for portfolio managers and analysts, the vocabulary of how markets actually move becomes essential. These six concepts govern how large institutions trade and manage risk — knowing them lets you ask sharper discovery questions and write more credible PRDs.

Volatility
The statistical measure of how much an asset's price fluctuates. The VIX — the "fear gauge" — tracks expected S&P 500 volatility. High volatility raises the value of real-time AI signals and stress-testing tools.
Leverage
Using borrowed capital to take positions larger than your equity allows. A 5× leveraged fund can amplify both gains and losses. AI risk tools that flag leverage exposure early are among the most commercially valuable in the space.
Correlation
How closely two assets move together. A correlation of +1 means they move in lockstep; -1 means opposite. Portfolio managers use correlation to diversify — AI is accelerating the detection of hidden correlations across large asset universes.
Arbitrage
Exploiting price discrepancies for the same asset across markets or time. Statistical arbitrage uses AI to identify subtle mispricings. Most pure arb has been captured by HFTs — but AI is opening new forms of it in less efficient markets.
Dark Pool
A private exchange where institutional investors trade large blocks without revealing their intent to public markets. Prevents price slippage. AI tools that infer dark-pool activity from public data are a growing product category.
Market Making
Providing continuous bid and ask prices to ensure market liquidity. Market makers profit from the bid-ask spread. AI is now core to automated market making — your tools operate in markets shaped by algorithmic liquidity provision.

Following the Mills Review, FCA executive Sheldon Mills has gone further: telling PYMNTS the government should examine whether large language models — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — should fall directly under FCA rules. Anthropic · Policy

Claude Fable 5 Returns — The First AI Model Ever Hit with US Export Controls

The US Department of Commerce lifted export controls on Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 on June 30, with global access restored July 1. The restrictions — imposed June 12 after Amazon researchers found a jailbreak bypassing cybersecurity safeguards — marked the first time the US government applied export controls directly to an AI model. Anthropic offered 50% of weekly usage limits at no charge through July 7 as a goodwill measure.

Why It Matters

The precedent is set: AI models can now be subject to the same export control regime as weapons systems. For any product relying on frontier models, geopolitical risk is now a legitimate PRD concern alongside latency and cost.
