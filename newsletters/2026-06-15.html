Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

This week's SpaceX listing — and the government order that followed barely a day later — are both, underneath the headlines, stories about who controls a company and who controls a model. Six terms for thinking about ownership, structure, and access.

Dual-Class Share Structure
SpaceX's IPO preserved Musk's 80%+ voting control via super-voting shares — a structure increasingly common when founders want public capital without ceding control.
Free Float
The portion of a company's shares actually available for public trading. A small free float, common in founder-controlled IPOs, can mean outsized price moves on relatively little volume.
Index Inclusion
When a stock joins a major index (S&P 500, Nasdaq-100), index funds must buy it regardless of valuation — a mechanical demand shock unrelated to fundamentals.
Primary vs. Secondary Shares
Primary shares are newly issued, raising cash for the company; secondary shares are sold by existing holders, cashing out. SpaceX's $75B raise was primarily primary shares.
Market Capitalization
Share price × shares outstanding. SpaceX's $2T+ close made it briefly bigger than Tesla — a number that says nothing about its $18.7B revenue or $4.9B net loss.
Export Controls
Government restrictions on who can access a technology, traditionally applied to hardware. This week's Anthropic order extended the concept to model access itself.

Cloud Security Alliance: 62% of Finance Firms Run AI Agents — Most Can't See What They're Doing

A 9 June CSA survey of 340 financial-services professionals found 62% have deployed AI agents, but governance hasn't kept pace — 20% reported known AI-security incidents and another 21% weren't sure. Action risk (63%) and human-authorization controls (53%) are now the top governance priorities.

Why It Matters

"Not sure if we've had an incident" is exactly what your product's audit logging exists to prevent. If your firm is in that 62%, the governance gap this survey describes is plausibly inside your own product.

Research & Think About
  • Ask security/compliance: would your firm know if your AI product's agents took an unintended action?
  • Read the CSA's "State of Cloud and AI for Financial Services 2026" report in full
  • Map "action risk" and "human authorization" — the survey's top two priorities — against your product's current design. Gaps?

US Orders Anthropic to Pull Its Two Most Powerful Models for Every Foreign User

On 13 June, the Commerce Department issued an export-control directive forcing Anthropic to disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for every foreign national worldwide — including its own staff — over a "narrow jailbreak" the government flagged. Claude Opus 4.8, Sonnet 4.6, and Haiku 4.5 remain unaffected. India, Anthropic's #2 market and home to its new TCS deal, is hit hardest.

Why It Matters

Model availability is now subject to export control, not just vendor policy. If your roadmap assumes access to a vendor's most capable model, you now need a plan for "the government revokes it overnight" — not just "the vendor deprecates it."

Research & Think About
  • Confirm which Claude models your product actually runs on — the affected tier, or the "remains operational" tier?
  • Read Anthropic's full statement and the CRS brief on federal-government/Anthropic dynamics — what's the disagreement really about?
  • Ask legal/procurement: does your vendor contract address a sudden government-mandated model withdrawal?

Global Regulators

FSB Tells Banks: Start Treating AI Agents Like "Synthetic Employees"

A 10 June Financial Stability Board consultation sets out 12 sound practices for agentic AI in finance — governance, lifecycle management, third-party risk. Its starkest line: since continuous human review of every agent decision is no longer practical, firms should adapt HR-style processes — boundaries, approvals, "onboarding" — to govern agents. Feedback due 22 July.

Why It Matters

"Adapt HR processes for AI agents" could appear verbatim in your product's governance section. If your tool deploys agents that act on analysts' behalf, this is the closest thing yet to a global regulatory expectation for managing them.

Research & Think About
  • Read the FSB consultation's 12 sound practices in full — which does your product already satisfy?
  • Sketch what an "onboarding" or "performance review" process for an AI agent in your product would actually look like
  • Compare FSB's framing to the SEC's 2026 exam priorities (see last issue) — converging or diverging standards?

ChatGPT Now "Dreams" — a Background Memory Rewrite With No User Input

Dreaming V3, rolled out to all ChatGPT tiers from 4 June, runs as a background process that continuously rewrites what ChatGPT remembers about a user — "going to Singapore in July" becomes "went to Singapore," unprompted, after the trip. Factual recall hit 82.8% at 5x compute efficiency, with new controls showing users what's been "remembered."

Why It Matters

Research & Think About

The Skill AI Can't Give You: Why 2026's Best PMs Are Protecting Their Judgment

A new Productside framework argues the PMs pulling ahead in 2026 aren't the most tool-fluent — they're the ones using AI to sharpen judgment rather than outsource it. The common failure mode: mastering prompting and agent tools, then using them to go faster at work you'd have done anyway, instead of work AI genuinely can't do.

Why It Matters

This directly challenges "use AI to write your PRD faster." The harder, more valuable question is whether AI changes what should be in the PRD at all — and that's a judgment call no model makes for you.

Research & Think About
  • Pick one recent task where you used AI to go faster — would going slower, with your own judgment, have changed the outcome?
