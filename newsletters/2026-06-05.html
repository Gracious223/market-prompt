Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Six terms used daily by the portfolio managers and analysts you build for. The faster you internalise this vocabulary, the more effectively you can turn their pain points into product decisions.

Yield Curve
The shape of interest rates across different maturities — short, medium, long. An inverted curve (short rates above long) historically signals recession. Macro-sensitive AI tools must account for regime changes a shifting yield curve signals.
P/E Ratio
Price divided by earnings per share — the most cited equity valuation metric. AI surfacing P/E must contextualise by sector: tech trades at very different multiples to utilities or financials. Raw numbers without context mislead.
Sector Rotation
The movement of capital between industry sectors as the economic cycle shifts. Energy often leads early recovery; tech late cycle. AI tools that detect or surface rotation signals are among the highest-value capabilities for macro PMs.
Sharpe Ratio
Risk-adjusted return: (Portfolio Return – Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation. The higher the Sharpe, the more return per unit of risk taken. PMs use it to compare strategies; your AI must surface it in context, not in isolation.
NAV (Net Asset Value)
Total assets minus liabilities, divided by units outstanding — the "true price" of a fund. Mutual funds publish NAV daily. ETFs trade on exchanges at prices that can deviate from NAV. A critical concept for any product touching fund data.
Custodian
The institution (usually a bank) that holds a fund's assets — handling safekeeping, settlement, and corporate actions. AI tools in post-trade workflows must interface with custodian data, often the most fragmented and least standardised source in the stack.
