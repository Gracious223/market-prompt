Lead Story · Cambridge CCAF

80% of Financial Institutions Are Deploying AI — But Only 14% See It as Transformational

The Cambridge Centre for Alternative Finance's 2026 Global AI in Financial Services Report — 628 institutions, 151 jurisdictions — finds 80% of financial firms deploying AI, yet only 14% see it as transformational. Fintechs lead incumbents in advanced AI adoption (47% vs. 30%) and in reaching a "transforming" stage (19% vs. 6%). Data quality, talent shortages, and legacy systems remain the biggest barriers to scale.

Why It Matters

Most financial institutions are using AI — but using and transforming are very different things. The 14% who see AI as transformational are the benchmark your product is measured against. Understanding what separates transformational adoption from surface-level deployment — integration depth, workflow fit, user trust — is the central product challenge for an AI PM in finance right now.

  • Ask your own users: does your product change how they work, or does it support tasks they'd do anyway? The gap between those answers is your product opportunity

Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today's stories cover market rotation, earnings data, risk metrics, and portfolio analysis — all using language your users take for granted. Six terms that directly surfaced in today's briefing, and that you need working fluency with to build credible AI products for buy-side analysts.

Yield Curve
The relationship between bond yields and their maturities. An inverted curve (short-term rates above long-term) has historically signalled recessions. AI macro models must correctly interpret yield curve signals to give analysts useful regime classification — the same task JPMorgan's agents tackled this week.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough decline in a portfolio's value. A key risk metric: a "20% maximum drawdown" means the portfolio fell 20% from its highest point. Controlling drawdown is often as important as generating return — and AI tools that surface drawdown risk in real time earn their seat at the table.
Basis Points (bps)
One basis point = 0.01% (one-hundredth of a percentage point). Universal in fixed income and macro. A 25bps rate cut means rates fell 0.25%. AI products touching interest rate or credit data must handle basis point precision — rounding errors at this scale move markets.
Earnings Season
The quarterly period when listed companies report financial results — revenue, earnings, and guidance. The data avalanche is the most time-compressed moment in an analyst's calendar. AI tools that process earnings releases at the moment they drop — before the broader market digests them — are among the highest-value applications in this space.
Short Selling
Borrowing and selling shares the seller doesn't own, hoping to buy them back cheaper later. A bearish bet. Short interest data is a key market signal: high short interest can validate bearish consensus or set up a short squeeze. AI tools that surface short interest alongside fundamental data give analysts a richer picture of market positioning.
Net Asset Value (NAV)
The per-share value of a fund's assets minus liabilities, calculated daily for mutual funds and ETFs. Understanding what moves NAV — and how to explain movements to clients — is foundational for any AI product that supports portfolio analysis or automated client reporting.

FCA's Mills Review: Seven Recommendations to Build an AI-Enabled Supervisory Model — 'Agentic Finance' Is Now a Regulatory Category

Published July 6, the FCA's Mills Review — the first AI regulatory review from any global financial regulator — forecasts 11 million UK adults delegating financial decisions to autonomous AI agents. Building now means helping define the standards. Waiting means complying with rules you had no input in shaping.

FCA CEO · Principles-Based

FCA CEO Rathi on CNBC: 'Not Going to Put in Lots of New, Detailed Rules on AI'

FCA CEO Nikhil Rathi told CNBC on July 2 the regulator will not introduce AI-specific rules. Consumer Duty, SM&CR, and operational resilience govern AI outputs — placing the compliance burden directly on firms to demonstrate good outcomes, not on regulators to define them. There is no AI rulebook to follow, only obligations to produce good outcomes.

Why It Matters

Principles-based regulation sounds permissive. It isn't. Without specific rules, your product must build its own compliance logic — and that logic will be tested in enforcement cases before clear AI guidance arrives. Explainability, audit logs, and documented decision rationale are not future requirements. They are current Consumer Duty obligations.

Research & Think About
  • Watch the CNBC interview with Nikhil Rathi — note specifically what he identifies as the FCA's priority questions for AI; these will shape guidance and enforcement priorities over the next 18 months
  • Research the difference between "prescriptive" and "principles-based" regulation in financial services — understanding this distinction is essential when briefing compliance teams on your product's risk profile
  • Ask your compliance team: if an AI output from your product caused a client to receive a bad outcome, who is accountable, under what FCA framework, and what evidence would exonerate your firm?

Skills · AI Literacy

AI Literacy Moves from Optional to Essential — The PM Skills Gap Is Now Measurable

The Institute of AI Product Management's 2026 analysis finds AI literacy — understanding model evaluation, embedding models, and reinforcement learning fundamentals — is now a differentiator in PM hiring and promotion. PMs who can participate in model evaluation design command a significant salary premium. The gap between AI-literate and AI-adjacent PMs is widening, not closing.

Why It Matters

"AI-adjacent" is not "AI-literate." Following AI news doesn't give you the foundation to challenge model assumptions, design evaluations, or identify failure modes — and those are the skills that determine whether an AI product succeeds in a regulated, high-stakes environment. Your physics and NLP background is a genuine head start; converting it into financial AI domain depth is the task.
