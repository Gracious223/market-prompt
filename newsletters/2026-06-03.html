Public Markets

Lead Story

Anthropic Files Confidential S-1: The Most Anticipated AI IPO Begins

Anthropic submitted a draft Form S-1 to the SEC on 1 June, targeting an October IPO at a $1.75–1.8 trillion valuation — potentially the largest public offering in history. The filing follows a $65B Series H at a $965B post-money valuation. Annualised revenue has crossed $47B, driven by enterprise Claude adoption for coding and agentic workflows.

Your analysts will model this name. The interesting product question: what does a defensible AI valuation actually rest on — revenue durability, compute contracts, or narrative? That framing is exactly the kind of judgement your tools should help surface, not bury.

Industry Research

Cambridge Judge: 81% of Firms Adopt AI — Only 14% Find It Transformational

The 2026 Global AI in Financial Services Report surveyed firms across 100+ countries. Fintechs lead incumbents in advanced AI (47% vs 30%). Key risk: 70% of regulators flag AI hallucinations as a top concern, yet only 18% of regulators are actively scaling AI themselves.

The 67-point gap between adoption and transformation is your product opportunity. Firms are buying AI but not building products that change how analysts actually work.

Regulation

FCA · UK

FCA Mills Review Reports This Summer — What the Board Hears Next

The Mills Review — examining how AI reshapes retail financial services — reports to the FCA Board in summer 2026. The FCA simultaneously expanded its Supercharged Sandbox to a second cohort and continues AI Live Testing. No AI-specific rules are planned: Consumer Duty and SM&CR remain the primary frameworks.

The Mills Report will set the tone for how regulators frame AI accountability in the UK. Its conclusions will shape every PRD written for a regulated firm in the next two years.

Parliamentary Pressure

Parliament Calls on FCA to Publish Comprehensive AI Guidance by Year-End

The Treasury Select Committee has called for the FCA to publish practical guidance on consumer protection rules as applied to AI, and on SM&CR accountability for senior managers, by end of 2026. Currently 48% of global regulators are still in the "exploring" stage of AI adoption, per Cambridge Judge.

Regulatory lag creates product risk. Building for SM&CR accountability before the guidance arrives is the kind of forward-thinking product design that protects your firm and your users.

AI Product Management

Skills & Strategy

HBR: Building AI PM Skills Is the Real Driver of Adoption — Not Better Technology

A February 2026 HBR piece makes the case that AI adoption stalls primarily because of PM skill gaps, not model limitations. 59% of product professionals say strategy and business acumen matter most; data literacy is now "non-negotiable." Over 70% of PMs use AI tools daily — but few use them to shape product direction.

The bottleneck is rarely the model. PMs who can frame the right problem, evaluate model outputs, and connect them to business outcomes are the scarcest resource in AI product right now.
