Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role — today's rotation

Six more terms your portfolio managers and analysts use daily. Each one connects directly to where AI is creating — or being asked to create — product value in the markets context.

Short Selling
Borrowing shares to sell immediately, aiming to buy them back cheaper later. Short sellers profit from price declines. AI tools now detect short signals by scanning for earnings-quality anomalies and sentiment divergence in filings.
Market Capitalisation
Total market value of outstanding shares (price × shares in issue). Large-cap (>$10B) stocks are liquid and well-covered; small-caps carry more risk and less analyst attention — where AI research tools can find genuine informational edge.
Earnings Call
A quarterly call where management presents results and fields analyst questions. AI tools now summarise transcripts, flag tone shifts versus prior quarters, and extract forward guidance signals in real time — a key analyst workflow.
Duration
A bond's price sensitivity to interest rate changes. Higher duration = larger price swings per rate move. Critical context for fixed-income portfolio managers; AI tools are used to model duration risk across multi-asset portfolios.
Liquidity
How easily an asset can be bought or sold without moving its price. Illiquid assets can trap capital when markets stress. Your AI tool must flag liquidity constraints when surfacing trade ideas or portfolio rebalancing suggestions.
Carry Trade
Borrowing at a low interest rate to invest in a higher-yielding asset, pocketing the spread. A classic macro strategy. Agentic AI is increasingly deployed to monitor carry unwind risk — sudden reversals can be violent and fast-moving.
