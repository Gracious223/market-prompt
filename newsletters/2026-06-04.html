Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Six more terms your portfolio managers use daily — and the product implications behind each one. Building fluency here means you'll ask better discovery questions and spot opportunities your competitors miss.

Beta
How much a stock moves relative to the market. Beta > 1 means more volatile; < 1 means more stable. AI tools use beta in real-time risk modelling, portfolio stress testing, and factor attribution.
Basis Points (bps)
One basis point = 0.01%. The standard unit in fixed income and rates. When your PM says "25bps cut," they mean 0.25%. AI tools that surface rate commentary must handle this precision correctly.
AUM (Assets Under Management)
The total market value of assets a fund or firm manages. A key sizing metric. A $10B AUM fund and a $100B fund have very different data needs, latency tolerances, and compliance obligations.
Bid-Ask Spread
The gap between what buyers will pay and sellers will accept. Tight spreads signal liquid, efficient markets. Wide spreads indicate risk or illiquidity — a critical signal your product may need to surface.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough decline in a portfolio's value over a period. Maximum drawdown is a core risk metric. AI tools that model scenarios for PMs must surface drawdown implications, not just expected returns.
Systematic vs. Discretionary
Systematic funds use rules and algorithms; discretionary funds rely on human judgment. Most AI tools either augment discretionary managers or power systematic strategies — knowing which you're building for shapes every product decision.

Joint Regulator Statement: Frontier AI Cyber Capabilities Now Exceed Skilled Practitioners — Boards Must Act

On 15 May 2026, the Bank of England, FCA, and HM Treasury issued a joint statement on frontier AI and cyber resilience — their strongest coordinated signal yet. Boards and senior management must understand frontier AI risks "sufficiently to set strategic direction." Investment and resourcing decisions must reflect the emerging threat, including exposure from end-of-life systems. Firms should review insurance coverage.

Why It Matters

This joint statement isn't guidance — it's a warning with named accountability under SM&CR. Senior managers are now explicitly responsible for understanding frontier AI risks. Any product you build that uses frontier models carries implicit regulatory exposure that your PRD must address from the start.

Research & Think About
