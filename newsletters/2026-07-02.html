Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

As AI spending hits $800 billion and AI stocks dominate quarterly returns, your analysts are navigating markets shaped by these concepts every day. Here are six terms you need in your vocabulary to build credibly for them.

Liquidity
How easily an asset can be bought or sold without moving its price. AI-driven trading has transformed liquidity in some markets — a critical variable when your product surfaces signals that may trigger trades.
Yield Curve
A chart plotting interest rates across bond maturities. Inverted yield curves historically signal recessions. Portfolio managers watch it obsessively — it's a macro signal your product may need to surface.
P/E Ratio
Price divided by earnings per share. The most basic equity valuation metric. AI stocks in Q2 reached elevated P/E ratios — exactly why Morningstar is calling overvaluation on hardware names like Micron.
ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)
A basket of securities traded like a single stock on an exchange. Thematic AI ETFs now give both retail and institutional investors broad AI exposure — understanding ETF flows is key market context.
Beta
Measures how much a stock moves relative to the broader market. A beta above 1 means more volatile. AI infrastructure stocks currently carry high beta — they amplify both market rallies and selloffs.
Free Cash Flow
Cash a company generates after capital expenditure. The metric equity analysts watch most closely when evaluating company health — and increasingly relevant as AI capex strains balance sheets.
