Anthropic Confidentially Files for IPO — Beating OpenAI to the Window

On 1 June, Anthropic confidentially filed IPO paperwork with the SEC, leapfrogging OpenAI to the milestone. The context is staggering: a $47B revenue run-rate (up from ~$30B earlier this year and $10B in annual revenue last year), driven largely by its Claude Code coding assistant, and a fresh $965B valuation versus OpenAI's $852B March mark. The private-market AI leaders are now racing to be priced in public.

Why it matters for you: This is the cleanest case study you'll get in how AI revenue durability gets scrutinised. Watch what the S-1 reveals about gross margins and customer concentration — those are the same questions your analysts ask of any AI name, and exactly the signals a good internal tool should surface fast.

Google Stock Slips on Plans to Raise $80B for AI

Google shares fell after it signalled plans to raise roughly $80B in debt to fund its AI infrastructure build-out — a follow-on to the capex anxiety that's been dogging the hyperscalers. Nvidia, meanwhile, buoyed the broader tech tape, with its CEO publicly backing Marvell. The split tape tells the story: investors love the chips, but are increasingly wary of who's borrowing to buy them.

Why it matters for you: The market is now separating "AI beneficiary" from "AI spender." That distinction is a product opportunity — a tool that maps a portfolio's exposure to AI capex vs. AI revenue is genuinely useful, and most generic dashboards don't do it.

SAP Ships a Full Suite of Agentic AI Tools for CFOs

SAP rolled out a broad set of agentic AI tools spanning most of corporate finance — cash management, tax, FP&A, billing, and governance. The flagship is a financial closing assistant that surfaces bottlenecks, automates postings and reconciliations, and resolves discrepancies in real time, with accuracy and auditability built in. Oracle made a parallel move extending its agentic platform into corporate banking.

Why it matters for you: Study SAP's framing — "accuracy and auditability" isn't marketing here, it's the core feature for finance buyers. This is the exact bar your internal tools must clear. Notice they shipped agents scoped to specific workflows (closing, billing) rather than one do-everything assistant — that's the design pattern that survives enterprise scrutiny.

Anthropic Expands Mythos to 150 Orgs Across Critical Infrastructure

Anthropic widened access to its high-capability Mythos model, bringing 150 additional partners across 15+ countries into Project Glasswing — spanning power, water, healthcare, communications, and hardware. The model's advanced capabilities (including on the cybersecurity side) mean access is being gated and rolled out deliberately rather than opened broadly.

Why it matters for you: Gated, sector-by-sector rollout is itself a product decision — Anthropic is treating capability as something to govern, not just ship. When you scope powerful AI for analysts, the access model and guardrails are part of the product, not an afterthought bolted on at launch.

OpenAI Buys Its Way Into Personal Finance With Hiro

OpenAI acquired Hiro, an AI personal-finance startup — a clear signal that the frontier labs are no longer content to sell raw model access and are moving up the stack into vertical applications. For anyone building a finance product on top of a foundation model, your platform partner is increasingly also a potential competitor.

Why it matters for you: This is the central build-vs-buy tension of AI PM right now: the model provider can absorb your use case. Your defensibility has to come from what they can't easily replicate — proprietary data, deep workflow integration, and the trust/compliance layer specific to your internal users. Thin wrappers over a model API are the most exposed.

"Tokenmaxxing" Is Fading — ROI Scrutiny Replaces Usage-at-All-Costs

A growing chorus of analysts say the era of "tokenmaxxing" — maximising AI/LLM usage for its own sake — is fading, as companies scrutinise return on investment and scale back after burning through annual AI budgets within months. Pair this with reports of OpenAI missing user and revenue targets, and the mood is shifting from "deploy everywhere" to "prove it pays."

Why it matters for you: The budget climate for internal AI tools is tightening. Build measurable value in from day one — time saved per analyst, decisions improved, errors caught. If you can't instrument the ROI, your project is exactly the kind being cut in this cycle. This is your sharpest near-term risk.
