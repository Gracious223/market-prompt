Public Markets

Lead Story

Morgan Stanley Opens Its $1.2 Trillion Wealth Funnel to Outside AI Agents

Morgan Stanley will let autonomous agents from corporations and other firms pull data directly from its ShareWorks and Equity Edge platforms — bypassing the human-built interface entirely. JPMorgan and Goldman, by contrast, are keeping agentic access internal for now.

This is the first credible move toward agent-to-agent finance at scale — your product may soon need to serve machine "users" with their own access patterns, not just human analysts.

IPO Watch

Anthropic Picks Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley and JPMorgan to Lead Its IPO

The trio will lead Anthropic's listing as early as October, at a $965B valuation. The same banks racing to sell AI tools to analysts are now underwriting the company that may define the model layer beneath those tools.

Watch how the prospectus frames revenue concentration and compute costs — it's the clearest public window you'll get into frontier-lab unit economics.

Market Structure

Goldman Sachs: The AI Rally Has Become "One Big Trade"

Strategist Ben Snider warns that gains are now so concentrated in AI and tech names that the market is effectively making a single bet — raising the odds of sharper volatility and weaker forward returns if sentiment turns.

Your users are navigating a market where "diversification" may be an illusion. Tools that surface concentration risk are more valuable than tools that chase momentum.

Agentic AI

Alpha Capture

Citadel Will Start Paying Rival Hedge Funds for Their Trading Ideas

Citadel's Global Quantitative Strategies arm is building a program to buy investment signals directly from other funds' discretionary managers — feeding human judgment into systematic models, an evolution of the 20-year-old "alpha capture" playbook.

This is human expertise being packaged as a data feed for AI systems — a pattern your product may need to replicate with your own analysts' judgment.

Read at Bloomberg →
Platform Shift

Apple Rebuilds Siri on Gemini — Then Lets You Replace It Entirely

Apple's overhauled Siri runs on a custom Gemini model under a roughly $1B/year licensing deal. But the bigger move is "Extensions" — letting users swap in ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude as their default assistant, breaking from Apple's closed-ecosystem instinct.

Apple just validated model-agnostic design at consumer scale — the same principle behind Goldman's internal AI Assistant. Vendor lock-in is becoming a strategic liability everywhere.

Regulation

SEC · US

SEC's 2026 Exam Priorities Now Weave AI Into Almost Every Category

AI is no longer its own line item — it's embedded across cybersecurity, emerging technology, automated investment tools, and operational resilience. Examiners will test whether AI-generated advice matches a client's stated strategy, and whether marketing claims about AI are accurate.

If your product touches recommendations or research, expect "does the output match the stated strategy" to become a formal compliance question, not just a UX one.

US Congress

House Draft Bill Would Set the First Federal Rules for Frontier AI

A new bipartisan draft would create federal pre-release review obligations for the most powerful models and attempt to settle the growing fight between state and federal AI rules — a direct response to the patchwork firms like yours currently navigate.

A federal framework would simplify — or freeze — the compliance landscape your product has to be built around. Either way, the ground is about to shift.
