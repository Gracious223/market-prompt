JPMorgan's AI Agents Beat the 60/40 Portfolio — But Its Own Strategists Say Don't Trust Them Yet

JPMorgan researchers built eight AI-powered investment agents using OpenAI and Anthropic models that classify market conditions across four regimes — Goldilocks, reflation, stagflation, and risk-off. Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Six terms you'll encounter when your analysts discuss how markets actually move — and where AI is starting to change the mechanics.

Dark Pools
Private trading venues where large block trades execute away from public exchanges. Banks and hedge funds use them to avoid moving the market. AI now helps detect when dark pool activity is signalling a major position change.
Market Maker
A firm that continuously quotes both a buy price and a sell price for a security, providing liquidity. The spread between those prices is their profit. AI is reshaping how market makers manage risk in real time.
Short Selling
Borrowing a security, selling it today, and buying it back later at (hopefully) a lower price. Shorts profit when prices fall. Analysts building bear cases for your tool will reference short interest as a key signal.
Basis Points (bps)
One basis point = 0.01%. Used for interest rates, spreads, and yield movements. A bond yield rising 25 bps means it moved up 0.25%. Your analysts will speak almost entirely in bps when discussing fixed income.
Earnings Per Share (EPS)
A company's net profit divided by shares outstanding. The single most watched analyst metric at earnings season. AI tools that help predict EPS surprises before the print are among the most commercially valuable in the market.
Active vs. Passive Management
Active managers pick individual securities to beat a benchmark; passive funds simply track an index (e.g. the S&P 500). Most of your users are active managers — they need an edge that passive investors by definition don't seek.
