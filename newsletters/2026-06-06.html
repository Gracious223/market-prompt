Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Six terms your portfolio managers and analysts use daily. As Anthropic nears a public listing and AI becomes structural in capital markets, fluency in this vocabulary is table stakes for building tools your users will trust.

Market Capitalisation
Share price × total shares outstanding. The headline measure of a company's size. Large-cap (£10B+), mid-cap, and small-cap carry different risk profiles, liquidity, and analyst coverage — all factors your product's filtering and ranking logic must handle correctly.
Beta
A stock's volatility relative to the broader market. Beta of 1 = moves with the market; >1 = more volatile; <1 = more stable. Risk models and portfolio construction tools depend on accurate beta — an AI output that gets this wrong leads to mispriced risk.
AUM (Assets Under Management)
The total market value of investments a fund or manager oversees on behalf of clients. The primary sizing metric for investment firms. AI tools are valued partly by the AUM they help manage more effectively — relevant when pitching internally.
Short Selling
Borrowing and selling a security you don't own, expecting the price to fall so you can buy it back cheaper. Short interest data is a rich alternative signal: high short interest can indicate institutional bearish conviction — a key input for sentiment-aware AI tools.
Derivatives
Contracts whose value is derived from an underlying asset — options, futures, swaps. Widely used for hedging and speculation. A major AI use case: pricing, structuring, and risk-managing derivatives at speed humans cannot match across complex portfolios.
Active vs. Passive Management
Active managers select individual securities to outperform a benchmark (alpha-seeking). Passive funds track an index. AI is reshaping both: it enhances active research velocity and enables smarter systematic factor exposure in passive strategies. Know which your users pursue.

FIS and Anthropic's jointly built agent automatically assembles evidence across core banking systems, evaluates activity against AML typologies, reduces false positives, and generates SAR narrative drafts. US Congress

The Great American AI Act: Bipartisan 269-Page Bill Would Freeze State Laws 3 Years and Require Pre-Release Model Submissions

Representatives Obernolte (R) and Trahan (D) released the Great American AI Act on June 4. Frontier AI developers must pre-submit new models for 30-day government review before release. All state AI laws are preempted for three years. The AI Safety Institute is formally established in statute with $100M annual authorisation.

Why It Matters

Three-year preemption of state laws simplifies your US compliance environment — but the federal floor rises. The 30-day model pre-submission requirement will directly affect how quickly new frontier model versions can be deployed in your product stack.

Research & Think About
  • Read Axios's summary of the Great American AI Act — the clearest single-source breakdown of what is in and what is out of the discussion draft
  • Understand the distinction between AI development regulation (primarily what this bill targets) and AI deployment regulation — your product is deployment
  • Research CAISI (formerly the AI Safety Institute) and what its 2026 testing mandate could mean for evaluation requirements on financial AI tools

Trump Signs AI Executive Order — Voluntary 30-Day Model Safety Review and New Federal AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse

Signed June 2, the executive order on "Advanced AI Innovation and Security" asks frontier labs to voluntarily submit powerful new models for government safety testing up to 30 days pre-release. It establishes a federal AI cybersecurity clearinghouse, directs agencies to build AI-specific benchmarks, and upgrades national security systems against AI-driven threats.

Governance · Product

Colorado Replaces Landmark AI Act with Lighter Disclosure Framework — A Live Case Study in AI Regulatory Evolution

Governor Polis signed SB 189 in May, replacing Colorado's original AI Act with a significantly lighter regime: no mandatory risk assessments, no annual reporting — just consumer disclosures, 30-day adverse outcome notices, and human review on request. Effective January 1, 2027. The original Act's deadline was June 30, 2026.

Why It Matters

Colorado's legislative reversal is a live lesson in AI regulatory instability. What looks like a compliance requirement one year can be rewritten the next. Build explainability and human review into your product not because it is legally required today — but because it will be, somewhere, eventually.
