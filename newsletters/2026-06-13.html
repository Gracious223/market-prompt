Public Markets

Lead Story

AI Chip Selloff Drags Nasdaq Into Its Worst Week Since April 2025

Broadcom's cautious AI chip outlook triggered a semiconductor rout in early June — the Nasdaq fell 4.18% in a single session, its worst day in over a year, and Micron lost roughly 20% over two days. The "AI infrastructure spend forever" assumption priced into chip valuations is being tested for the first time.

Your firm's AI roadmap is likely funded by the same capex cycle the market is now questioning. If chip spending growth merely slows rather than collapses, the gap between "story" and "reality" is where your analysts will spend the next quarter.

Research Tools

Bloomberg Maps Out a Future Where the Terminal Talks Back

Bloomberg's April roadmap shows ASKB evolving from a natural-language search layer into a full agentic engine — assembling "pre-earnings prep" or "post-earnings analysis" workflows automatically from filings, news, and Bloomberg Intelligence research in minutes.

ASKB is the incumbent's answer to AlphaSense and Hebbia. If Bloomberg bundles agentic workflows into a terminal analysts already pay for, that's the bar your internal tool needs to clear or sidestep.

IPO Watch

Anthropic and OpenAI Both Quietly File for IPOs — Within a Week of Each Other

Anthropic confidentially filed on 1 June targeting a ~$965B valuation, days after a $65B raise. OpenAI followed on 8 June, reportedly targeting ~$850B. Both could list as early as October — alongside SpaceX.

When the company behind your product's underlying model also becomes a stock your PMs cover, the line between "vendor" and "holding" blurs — expect questions about Anthropic's revenue concentration in your own product reviews.

Agentic AI

Hedge Funds

The First Fully Agentic Hedge Fund Just Opened Its Doors

Lumenai Investments launched the Lumenai Innovation Fund around 1 June — a global equity long-short strategy where AI agents are the decision-makers, not just research tools, aiming for returns uncorrelated to traditional markets.

This is the live experiment your risk committee will eventually ask about: what happens when "the analyst" is an agent with no human sign-off on individual trades? Your product's audit trail needs to answer that before someone asks.

Internal Tooling

Two Sigma Is Teaching Its AI Tools to Understand Two Sigma

Beyond using frontier models for research, Two Sigma is making its AI tools "firm-aware" — able to navigate internal platforms, production environments, incident management, and business processes, not just public data.

"Firm-aware" is the unglamorous, high-value version of agentic AI — and it's exactly the lane your internal product sits in. The moat isn't the model; it's how deeply it understands your firm's own systems.

Regulation

Bank of England

The Bank of England Is Now Stress-Testing AI Agents for "Herding" Risk

The BoE confirmed it's building AI scenarios into financial stability stress tests, focused on whether multiple AI trading systems could react identically to the same signal and amplify a sell-off — though its April FPC record found no current evidence of systemic risk.

"Herding" is a direct critique of any tool that nudges many analysts toward the same conclusion at once. If your product surfaces the same signal to every PM simultaneously, you may be contributing to the exact risk the BoE is modelling.

Read at Bloomberg →
SEC · US

SEC's Own Advisory Committee Wants Clearer AI Disclosure Rules

The SEC's Investor Advisory Committee has recommended formal disclosure guidelines for how firms describe AI use to investors — distinct from the 2023 predictive-analytics rule proposal that the Investment Adviser Association has urged the SEC to withdraw entirely.

"AI-washing" disclosure rules will eventually apply to how your firm describes its own AI products internally and to clients — get ahead of it by being precise about what your tool does today, not what's on the roadmap.

From the Labs

Anthropic

Anthropic Ships Claude Fable 5 — Its Most Capable Public Model Yet, With New Guardrails Attached

Fable 5, a public release of the "Mythos-class" Claude Mythos model, brings major gains in reasoning and coding but ships with guardrails that actively block high-risk cybersecurity and biology queries — activating in under 5% of sessions, per Anthropic.

A model powerful enough to need built-in refusal behaviour is powerful enough to materially change what your analysts can do unsupervised — worth testing Fable 5 directly against your current eval suite.

Microsoft

Microsoft's First Homegrown Reasoning Model Claims It Beats Claude Sonnet 4.6 — at a Tenth of the Cost

MAI-Thinking-1, a 35B-parameter model built without OpenAI data, scored 97% on AIME 2025 and was preferred over Claude Sonnet 4.6 in blind human evaluations across 1,276 tasks — while Microsoft claims roughly 10x better cost efficiency.

"10x cheaper, slightly preferred" is a more dangerous competitive claim than "better." If even partially true, it changes the cost-per-query maths behind every model-routing decision your engineers make.

AI Evals Are Becoming THE Skill for Product Managers in 2026

Industry training is converging on one message: PMs who can design evaluation criteria — offline tests, A/B experiments, edge cases, ongoing monitoring — for AI features are the ones getting hired at a premium, ahead of pure coding ability.

This is the single most actionable skill gap for you right now. Every story in this issue — Fable 5's guardrails, MAI-Thinking-1's benchmarks, Lumenai's agentic trades — reduces to "how do you evaluate whether this output is good enough?"

A widely-read guide argues AI fluency has shifted from differentiator to baseline — the new gap is between PMs who can write a prompt and those who can design the guardrails, fallback behaviour, and cost models around it.

Fallback behaviour and cost modelling are exactly the gaps between "demo" and "production" for internal tools — and they're the parts most generalist PMs skip. This is where your attention to detail is a genuine edge.
