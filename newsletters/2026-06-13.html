Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

This week's chip selloff is also a vocabulary lesson. Here are six terms an analyst would reach for to explain what just happened to Micron, and why a Fed rate decision matters to a portfolio full of AI infrastructure names.

Beta
A measure of a stock's volatility relative to the broader market. Micron's ~20% two-day drop reflects a high-beta stock amplifying a sector-wide move — AI-adjacent names tend to run high beta in both directions.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough decline in an investment's value before a new high is reached. Analysts track "maximum drawdown" to size downside risk — exactly what this week's chip selloff produced for AI-heavy portfolios.
Liquidity
How easily an asset can be bought or sold without moving its price. In a fast selloff, liquidity can evaporate precisely when investors need it most — a key input to the BoE's "herding" stress-test work (see Regulation).
Yield Curve
The relationship between bond yields and their maturities. A spike in Treasury yields after May's jobs report raises borrowing costs and pressures growth stocks valued on far-future earnings — like most AI names.
Short Selling
Borrowing and selling a security you don't own, betting its price falls, then buying it back cheaper. Crowded "long AI" positioning meant relatively few investors were short going into this selloff — one reason the move was so sharp.
Basis Points (bps)
One hundredth of a percentage point (0.01%) — the standard unit for rate moves and spreads. When PMs discuss the Fed "hiking 25bps," this is the unit; useful shorthand you'll hear constantly.

Regulation
Bank of England

The Bank of England Is Now Stress-Testing AI Agents for "Herding" Risk

The BoE confirmed it's building AI scenarios into financial stability stress tests, focused on whether multiple AI trading systems could react identically to the same signal and amplify a sell-off — though its April FPC record found no current evidence of systemic risk.

Why It Matters

"Herding" is a direct critique of any tool that nudges many analysts toward the same conclusion at once. If your product surfaces the same signal to every PM simultaneously, you may be contributing to the exact risk the BoE is modelling.

Research & Think About
  • Read the BoE's April 2026 FPC record in full — note how they define "herding" precisely
  • Ask: does your product ever surface identical recommendations to multiple users at once? What's the risk if it does?
  • Learn what a "system-wide exercise" stress test is and how it differs from a single-firm stress test
