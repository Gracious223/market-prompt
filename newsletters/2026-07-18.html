Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today's lead story turns on AuM, NAVs, waterfall distributions, and custodians — the operational vocabulary of asset management. Here are six terms that will help you discuss BCG's findings and Moody's agent use cases with portfolio managers and engineers credibly.

Assets Under Management (AuM)
The total market value of assets a fund or firm manages on behalf of clients. BCG's $147T figure represents all global AuM. It is the primary scale metric for the industry and drives revenue via management fees (typically 0.1–1% of AuM annually).
Net Asset Value (NAV)
A fund's total assets minus liabilities, divided by shares outstanding. Calculated daily for mutual funds, periodically for alternatives. NAV reconciliation — ensuring records match across custodians and administrators — is a major operational pain point that AI agents are beginning to automate.
Waterfall Distribution
The structured order in which profits from a private markets investment are distributed — first returning capital to LPs, then preferred returns, then carried interest to the GP. Complex waterfall calculations are a primary private markets use case for AI agents, per both BCG and Moody's.
Custodian
A financial institution that holds a fund's assets on its behalf — safeguarding securities, settling trades, and maintaining records. Large funds use multiple custodians across geographies. AI agents reconciling NAVs "across custodians" means reading and matching records from multiple of these institutions simultaneously.
Sharpe Ratio
A measure of risk-adjusted return: excess return above the risk-free rate divided by the portfolio's standard deviation. Higher Sharpe = more return per unit of risk taken. When JPMorgan's AI agents "beat the 60/40 with lower volatility," they were generating a better Sharpe ratio — the key metric portfolio managers care about.
Operational Alpha
Competitive advantage generated through operational efficiency rather than investment skill. BCG's thesis is that AI will shift the source of advantage from investment alpha (picking better stocks) to operational alpha (running a more efficient, scalable, AI-powered platform). This reframes where your product creates value.

The US Treasury's Financial Services AI Risk Management Framework (FS AI RMF), developed with over 100 financial institutions and aligned to NIST, is now the sector's operative compliance standard. The framework covers 230 Control Objectives across the full AI risk lifecycle — from governance and data quality through fraud detection, identity, and explainability.
