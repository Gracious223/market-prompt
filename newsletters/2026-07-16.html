Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today's record earnings at Morgan Stanley and BlackRock are packed with vocabulary your users take for granted. Here are six concepts — all new to this series — that will help you read these results and the conversations around them with genuine fluency.

Equities Trading Revenue
Revenue generated by institutional trading desks executing large share orders for hedge funds, asset managers, and other institutional clients. Morgan Stanley's $6.3B record result reflects the surge in AI-sector trading volumes — your users' decisions create that revenue.
Investment Banking (IBD)
Broader than M&A advisory: encompasses equity underwriting (IPOs, follow-on offerings), debt underwriting (bond issuances), and advisory on restructurings. Morgan Stanley's IBD revenue surged 58% — largely driven by AI-sector companies raising capital.
P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)
A company's share price divided by its earnings per share. A P/E of 30 means investors pay £30 for every £1 of earnings. AI-infrastructure stocks carry high P/Es because investors price in high future growth — your analysts monitor and debate P/E multiples constantly.
Net Revenue (Bank Reporting)
For financial firms, net revenue subtracts interest expense from gross revenue to reflect true economic value generated. When Goldman reports $20.3B and Morgan Stanley reports $21.3B, these are net figures — the basis for compensation, investment, and ROI decisions.
CTP Regime (Critical Third Party)
A UK regulatory framework that designates major infrastructure providers — including AI and cloud vendors — for direct FCA/PRA oversight. Under the Mills Review, HM Treasury must designate major AI providers as CTPs by end-2026. Any firm using Claude, GPT, or Gemini in regulated workflows is affected.
Basis Points (bps)
One hundredth of one percentage point (0.01%). Used universally in fixed income, rates, spreads, and fee discussions. A 25bps rate cut equals 0.25%. If your product surfaces yield data, fee comparisons, or interest rate outputs, expect users to communicate everything in basis points — not percentages.

FCA · Mills Review · Published 6 Jul 2026

FCA Mills Review Published: 7 Recommendations — Accountability Does Not Shift When AI Takes on the Work

The FCA's landmark Mills Review — led by Executive Director Sheldon Mills — found that accountability in retail financial services "does not shift" as AI takes on more decision-making. Its 7 recommendations include scaling the FCA's AI Lab, enabling agentic finance infrastructure, and directing HM Treasury to designate major AI providers as Critical Third Parties by end-2026. 20% of consumers said they would be likely to use autonomous AI within preset goals.

Why It Matters

The Mills Review is now the definitive regulatory frame for your work. When your agentic product acts on behalf of a client, the accountability question — who is responsible when it goes wrong — has now been answered: the firm that deployed it, not the model provider.

Research & Think About

  • Map each of the 7 recommendations to a decision that your product team owns — which ones require a change to how you design, test, or document AI features?
