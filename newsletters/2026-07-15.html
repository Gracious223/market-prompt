Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Today's stories are full of terms your users take for granted — from Goldman's record EPS beat to LinqAlpha's $5T AUM client base. Here are six concepts you need to own to sound credible in the room.

EPS (Earnings Per Share)
Net profit divided by shares outstanding. The headline metric every analyst watches on earnings day. Goldman's $20.98 EPS vs. a $14.48 estimate is why today's result is a market event.
Consensus Estimate
The aggregated analyst forecast for a company's earnings or revenue. When Goldman "beats consensus," it means actuals came in above this average. Beating or missing the consensus — not the prior year — is what moves the stock.
AUM (Assets Under Management)
The total value of capital a fund or firm actively manages. LinqAlpha's 70+ clients manage $5T+ AUM. AUM is how the buy side measures itself — larger AUM means more at stake in every data and tooling decision.
M&A Advisory
The service investment banks provide to companies buying or selling other businesses — structuring deals, running valuations, managing negotiations. Goldman's record quarter was driven partly by surging AI-sector M&A activity.
Forward Guidance
Management's forecast for future revenue or earnings. Often moves a stock more than the actual result. AI is increasingly used to detect shifts in guidance language across earnings transcripts — a core analyst workflow.
Earnings Season
The four-to-six-week window each quarter when S&P 500 companies report results. We're in one now — today alone brings Goldman, Morgan Stanley, BlackRock, and JNJ. Your analysts' workload spikes during this period, which shapes what features matter most.

NRF: What Agentic AI in Finance Actually Means for Compliance Teams

Norton Rose Fulbright's July analysis translates the FCA's Mills Review into compliance obligations: consumer duty still applies when an agent acts on a customer's behalf; SM&CR accountability attaches to whoever defines the agent's objectives; and bias in AI outputs is already a regulatory issue under existing FCA principles — no new rules required.

Why It Matters

The Mills Review sets the direction; this piece shows what it means for product builders today. If your agent makes a recommendation that causes consumer harm, the accountability question under SM&CR lands with the person who signed off the agent's design — which may include you.

Research & Think About
