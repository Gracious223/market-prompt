Public Markets 101
Essential concepts for your role

Six more terms your portfolio managers and analysts use daily — rotated from previous editions. As agentic tools move deeper into research and execution, fluency in this vocabulary is what lets you ask the right question when an output looks "off."

Volatility (VIX)
A statistical measure of how much an asset's price swings over time, often tracked via the VIX — the market's "fear index". AI risk models increasingly forecast volatility regimes in real time, a core input for any portfolio construction tool.
Market Maker
A firm that continuously quotes both buy and sell prices for a security, providing liquidity in exchange for the spread. Many market-making desks now run AI-driven quoting engines — relevant when analysts ask about market microstructure.
Dividend Yield
Annual dividend per share divided by share price, expressed as a percentage. A core input to income-investing strategies. Any AI screening tool that ranks "value" or "income" opportunities lives or dies on getting this — and trailing-vs-forward — right.
Free Cash Flow (FCF)
Cash a company generates after operating expenses and capital spending — the cash actually available to shareholders. Many AI-driven valuation models use FCF as their foundation; it's the number analysts check first if your product touches valuation.
Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)
A basket of securities trading on an exchange like a single stock, usually tracking an index. ETFs are a major battleground for "AI-thematic" investing — and a useful lens for how your users think about diversified exposure versus single-name bets.
Limit Order
An instruction to buy or sell at a specified price or better, as opposed to a market order executed immediately at the best available price. Execution-focused AI agents must understand this distinction precisely — it is "a good price" versus "this price."
